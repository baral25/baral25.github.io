About MealMap

Frequently constrained by tight budgets and confronting academic pressures, students are now recognised as a demographic disproportionately affected by food and nutritional challenges

Initially tasked with developing a platform for the Pantry through which current Curtin University Bentley campus students who were unable to access the pantry could access information on Food and Relief services around Curtin, our porject took us beyond our scope identifying the underlying issues.

The Project

Initially, our project team's primary objective was to create a platform that streamlines the process of students accessing essential relief food services. To achieve this, we embarked on a journey of research and collaboration with external charitable organizations such as St. Vincent de Paul Society WA, Food Bank WA, and Lifestreams Christian Church. Our initial findings revealed that simply delivering a platform would not be sufficient to improve the challenging circumstances faced by students seeking efficient food assistance. We recognized a crucial need to raise awareness about the processes involved in accessing relief food services. These services often rely on word-of-mouth communication within the community, making it challenging for students, especially those from diverse cultural and religious backgrounds, to navigate the available resources. Our project aimed to bridge this information gap by highlighting the food relief services available in the vicinity of Curtin University Bentley Campus, ensuring that students facing food insecurity challenges can easily explore their options. Furthermore, we noted that the official websites of these charitable organizations often provided limited information, primarily focusing on Australian documentation requirements such as Centerlink. However, some of these charities could offer assistance without these documents, making them accessible to international students as well. Meal Map was developed to showcase these inclusive charities, offering a path to efficient access without documentation barriers. Additionally, Meal Map provides guidance on food relief services that domestic students may approach, emphasizing the need for the required Australian documents.
