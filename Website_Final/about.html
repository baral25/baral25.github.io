The Project

We embarked on a mission to address the pressing issue of food insecurity among students, particularly within the Curtin University community. Recognizing that the process of obtaining relief food services was often complex and fragmented, our project team set out to streamline and simplify this journey for students. We collaborated with external charitable organizations, including St. Vincent de Paul Society WA, Food Bank WA, and Lifestreams Christian Church, to gain a comprehensive understanding of the challenges faced by students seeking food assistance. What we discovered was the need not only for a technological solution but also for increased awareness about available relief services. The project's centerpiece, Meal Map, was designed to bridge these gaps, making it easier for students, regardless of their cultural or religious backgrounds, to access essential food resources. Meal Map goes beyond being a platform; it is a resource that empowers students to find and connect with inclusive food relief services efficiently, ultimately fostering a more supportive and inclusive community at Curtin University.
